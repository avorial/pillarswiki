# Timeline

This master timeline preserves the structured event data from the Pillars of Salt chronicle source. Individual event pages have not been split out in this first CampaignRepo pass.

## Prehistory and Mythic Foundations

**Range:** 1800 BC-1827

Los Angeles begins as a contested sacred landscape before the Kindred ever arrive. The oldest supernatural claims center on Catalina Island, Tongva sacred practice, and Earthbound attention that will later pull Demon politics into the city.

### 1800 BC - Catalina's First Infernal Shadow

Tongva sacred work on Catalina Island becomes entangled with the Earthbound Enshagkushanna. A Dreamspeaker's fall gives the island a hidden infernal inheritance long before Los Angeles exists as a modern city.

- Spheres: Demon, Mage, Indigenous Myth
- Factions: [[Tongva]], [[Enshagkushanna Cult]]
- Locations: [[Catalina Island]]
- NPCs: [[Enshagkushanna]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Explains why Catalina remains a pressure point for infernal cults and Earthbound attention.

## Arrival of the Kindred

**Range:** 1828-1916

The first Cainites arrive through shipwreck, patronage, and ambition. Houghton, Murietta, Don Sebastian, and Fortier become the foundation stones for a city that has not yet decided whether it belongs to princes, barons, monsters, or the dream factory.

### 1828 - The Danube Wrecks in San Pedro Bay

Christopher Houghton reaches Los Angeles by disaster rather than conquest when the Danube wrecks near San Pedro. Don Sebastian shelters the survivors, unknowingly opening the door to the city's first vampire power line.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Early Kindred]], [[Don Sebastian's Household]]
- Locations: [[San Pedro Bay]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Christopher Houghton]], [[Don Sebastian]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Establishes Houghton as the first Cainite anchor for the local timeline.

### 1853 - Joaquin Murietta is Embraced

Houghton expands his influence by Embracing Joaquin Murietta. The act ties Los Angeles' undead history to outlaw legend, frontier violence, and a city still becoming itself.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Early Kindred]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Christopher Houghton]], [[Joaquin Murietta]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Adds a legendary local figure to the city's Cainite ancestry.

### 1870 - Don Sebastian Enters the Blood

Houghton Embraces Don Sebastian, transforming a mortal host into one of the key undead rulers of early Los Angeles. The gesture creates a future prince and a future revolutionary target.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Early Kindred]], [[Camarilla]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Christopher Houghton]], [[Don Sebastian]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Sets up the Camarilla order that the Anarch Revolt will later overthrow.

### 1909 - Houghton Discovers the Moving Image

Houghton sees a motion picture and recognizes an industry that can shape appetite, fantasy, and memory. Hollywood becomes more than a mortal business; it becomes an undead obsession.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Hollywood Interests]]
- Locations: [[Hollywood]]
- NPCs: [[Christopher Houghton]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Frames Hollywood as a strategic supernatural asset from the beginning.

### 1912 - Louis Fortier Moves West

Louis Fortier settles in Los Angeles and begins building the influence that will later let him survive regime changes, invasions, and alliances that destroy less adaptable elders.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Independent Kindred]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Louis Fortier]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Introduces one of the city's durable power brokers.

## Hollywood, Sabbat Infiltration, and Early Power Plays

**Range:** 1917-1942

The entertainment industry becomes a supernatural engine. Garou, Fallen, Sabbat agents, and ambitious Lasombra recognize that Hollywood can change mortal belief at continental scale.

### 1917 - Glass Walkers Enter the City

Don Leandro and his Glass Walker pack arrive in Los Angeles, adding Garou attention to the city's industrial, media, and urban spirit landscape.

- Spheres: Werewolf
- Factions: [[Glass Walkers]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Don Leandro]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Makes the city a cross-sphere battleground before the Anarch Free State exists.

### 1927 - The Caballero Claims Southern California

A Lasombra known as the Caballero is named Archbishop of Southern California. His campaign for Los Angeles includes grooming Leila Monroe as a ghoul and preparing a longer Sabbat infiltration strategy.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Lasombra]]
- Locations: [[Southern California]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[The Caballero]], [[Leila Monroe]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Creates the Sabbat line that will haunt Anarch Los Angeles for decades.

### 1930s - The Morningstar Hides in the Dream Factory

After escaping the Abyss, Lucifer finds shelter in Los Angeles and sees entertainment as a lever for shaping the world. His presence draws Earthbound attention that will later erupt into open catastrophe.

- Spheres: Demon
- Factions: [[Fallen]], [[Earthbound]]
- Locations: [[Hollywood]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Lucifer]], [[Enshagkushanna]], [[Dagon]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Makes the entertainment industry central to Demon politics in LA.

### 1942 - Leila Monroe and the Sabbat's Quiet War

Leila Monroe is Embraced as the Sabbat adopts a deeper infiltration posture toward the Anarchs. The sect prepares to survive by hiding inside the city's revolutionary mythology.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Anarchs]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Leila Monroe]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Seeds later Sabbat survival networks inside Anarch territory.

## The Anarch Free State

**Range:** 1943-1967

MacNeil's arrival, Don Sebastian's destruction, and the Status Perfectus create the Free State. The new order is never uncontested: Sabbat infiltration, Camarilla observers, and hidden monsters all test its borders.

### 1943 - Jeremy MacNeil Arrives

Jeremy MacNeil enters Los Angeles one year before the city erupts. His presence gives the coming revolt a center of gravity and a name that will outlast the revolution itself.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Jeremy MacNeil]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Introduces the face of the Free State.

### 1944 - The Second Anarch Revolt

Don Sebastian meets Final Death and Camarilla authority collapses in Los Angeles. The Anarch Free State rises from the wreckage as both a political experiment and an unresolved argument.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]], [[Camarilla]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Don Sebastian]], [[Jeremy MacNeil]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Defines LA as the great Anarch symbol of the western nights.

### 1945 - The Status Perfectus is Drafted

The Revolutionary Council attempts to turn revolt into governance by drafting the Status Perfectus. The document gives the Free State ideals, even as the city remains too fractured to fully embody them.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]], [[Revolutionary Council]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Jeremy MacNeil]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Provides the ideological standard later factions claim, distort, or betray.

### 1947 - The Revolutionary Council Dissolves

The council steps aside, leaving Los Angeles to its barons, ideals, grudges, and personal power blocs. Freedom survives, but so does fragmentation.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]], [[Revolutionary Council]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: None listed
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Explains why the Free State later struggles to answer coordinated threats.

### 1952 - Victor Girard Becomes the Press Liaison

Victor Girard, childe of Don Sebastian, reveals himself to MacNeil and brokers a role as Kindred liaison to mortal press interests. The old regime's blood finds a place inside the new order.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]], [[Camarilla Remnants]], [[Mortal Press]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Victor Girard]], [[Don Sebastian]], [[Jeremy MacNeil]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Shows how old Camarilla lineages survive through utility and compromise.

### 1955 - The Failed Contract on MacNeil

Clan Assamite accepts a Camarilla Inner Circle contract on Jeremy MacNeil. MacNeil survives with Crispus Attucks' help, and the same year Disneyland opens, giving the city another symbolic dream machine.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Assamites]], [[Camarilla]], [[Anarchs]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Anaheim]]
- NPCs: [[Jeremy MacNeil]], [[Crispus Attucks]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Reinforces the Free State's legend while tying mortal fantasy economies into the setting.

### 1960 - Amethyst Rules Santa Ana

Amethyst becomes Baron of Santa Ana, strengthening Orange County's place in the broader Los Angeles political map.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]]
- Locations: [[Santa Ana]], [[Orange County]]
- NPCs: [[Amethyst]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Sets up Orange County as more than a background suburb.

### 1962 - Carolina Valez Takes the Archdiocese

Carolina Valez becomes Los Angeles' Archbishop, giving the Sabbat a more capable local center of gravity beneath the Free State's surface.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Carolina Valez]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]], [[Montreal by Night]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Connects LA Sabbat history to later Montreal continuity.

### 1963 - David Geduld Watches for the Tower

David Geduld, a Ventrue Camarilla spy, moves into Los Angeles. The Free State may have rejected princes, but the Ivory Tower never stops taking notes.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Ventrue]], [[Anarchs]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[David Geduld]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Gives the Camarilla an intelligence thread inside Anarch territory.

### 1963 - The Caballero's Bid Fails

The Caballero leaves the archbishopric to pursue a western cardinalship and is destroyed by a Court of Blood. His fall clears space for later Sabbat leadership struggles.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Lasombra]]
- Locations: [[Southern California]]
- NPCs: [[The Caballero]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Explains a Sabbat succession gap in Southern California.

### 1965 - The Sabbat Siege and the Watts Fire

The Sabbat launches a major push into Los Angeles and fails. Mohammed al-Muthlim changes sides during the fighting, hiding beneath an Anarch mask while rising as a Sabbat bishop.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Anarchs]], [[Crypt's Sons]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Watts]]
- NPCs: [[Mohammed al-Muthlim]], [[Archbishop Fuller]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Binds Sabbat infiltration to LA's mortal unrest and Anarch vulnerabilities.

## Crossover Pressure and the Hidden Wars

**Range:** 1968-1997

Los Angeles becomes a pressure cooker for Earthbound schemes, Setite cults, Changeling courts, Sabbat politics, street wars, and Asian supernatural strategy. By the late 1990s, the Free State is already weaker than its legend.

### 1969 - Murder, Serpents, and Street Wars

The Earthbound Manishtusu comes hunting Lucifer and works through gang violence. In the same period, Dawn Cavanaugh establishes a Temple of Set in Hollywood, adding another predatory faith to the city.

- Spheres: Demon, Vampire
- Factions: [[Earthbound]], [[Followers of Set]], [[Hollywood Temple of Set]]
- Locations: [[Hollywood]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Manishtusu]], [[Lucifer]], [[Dawn Cavanaugh]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]], [[LA by Night]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Turns the city's street violence and entertainment culture into overlapping occult fronts.

### 1972 - California Becomes Pacifica

The Changelings name California the Kingdom of Pacifica under King Sean. LA's supernatural map gains another courtly layer that rarely aligns with Kindred borders.

- Spheres: Changeling
- Factions: [[Kingdom of Pacifica]]
- Locations: [[California]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[King Sean]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Adds Changeling sovereignty to the city's already crowded occult geography.

### 1974 - Double Helix Takes Torrance

Bishop Mark and his Blood Brother pack claim Torrance, making the South Bay a defined Sabbat pressure point beneath the wider Anarch Free State.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Double Helix]], [[Blood Brothers]]
- Locations: [[Torrance]]
- NPCs: [[Bishop Mark]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Creates a local domain hook for Sabbat body-horror plots.

### 1982 - Valez Wins the Sabbat Argument

Carolina Valez challenges Archbishop Fuller, and Cardinal Strathcona removes Fuller as a failure. Valez keeps the Sabbat underground for the next decade instead of wasting strength on open war.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Carolina Valez]], [[Archbishop Fuller]], [[Cardinal Strathcona]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]], [[Montreal by Night]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Explains why the Sabbat survives in LA by patience rather than spectacle.

### 1985 - Aeron ap Fiona Takes Pacifica

King Sean is murdered by the Radical People's Front. Queen Aeron ap Fiona takes the throne young, and her later decadence complicates Changeling stability in the region.

- Spheres: Changeling
- Factions: [[Kingdom of Pacifica]], [[Radical People's Front]]
- Locations: [[California]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[King Sean]], [[Queen Aeron ap Fiona]], [[Hamal]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Gives the Changeling court its own internal fracture during LA's late Free State years.

### 1992 - The Rodney King Riots

The city convulses in mortal unrest, and every hidden faction reads opportunity or warning in the fires. The Sabbat later judges its leadership by whether it exploited the moment.

- Spheres: Mortal, Vampire
- Factions: [[Anarchs]], [[Sabbat]], [[Camarilla Observers]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Carolina Valez]], [[Mohammed al-Muthlim]]
- Sources: [[LA by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Becomes a political test for Sabbat ambition and Anarch control.

### 1993 - Valez is Exiled

Carolina Valez is punished for not using the riots to take Los Angeles. Mohammed al-Muthlim becomes Archbishop, while Valez is later placed over Montreal.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Sabbat]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Mexico]], [[Montreal]]
- NPCs: [[Carolina Valez]], [[Mohammed al-Muthlim]]
- Sources: [[Montreal by Night]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Links Los Angeles Sabbat failure to Montreal's later sect politics.

### 1997 - The Extraordinary Commission Looks East to West

Liejie Li takes command of the Extraordinary Commission with Chiu Bao as her militant first officer. Their mandate is to crack Los Angeles, then use the beachhead against San Diego and San Francisco.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin
- Factions: [[Extraordinary Commission]], [[Quincunx]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[San Diego]], [[San Francisco]]
- NPCs: [[Liejie Li]], [[Chiu Bao]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Sets the strategic logic for the Great Leap Outward.

## The Great Leap Outward and Fall of LA

**Range:** 1998-2001

The Kuei-jin invasion, Wraithly disasters, Sabbat survival rites, and Camarilla containment strategy tear apart the Free State. The New Promise Mandarinate emerges from exhaustion, collaboration, and divide-and-rule politics.

### 1998 - The Great Leap Outward Hits Los Angeles

Kuei-jin forces strike the city's unprepared Kin-jin and initially tear through independent vampires and smaller gangs. Resistance hardens around MacNeil, Fortier, Salvador Garcia, and Crispus Attucks.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin, Vampire
- Factions: [[Kuei-jin]], [[Anarchs]], [[New Promise Mandarinate]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Jeremy MacNeil]], [[Louis Fortier]], [[Salvador Garcia]], [[Crispus Attucks]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Begins the military collapse of the Anarch Free State.

### 1998 - Elijah's Burial Rite

The Sabbat prophet Elijah bargains with Manishtusu after foreseeing the sect's destruction in the invasion. A survival ritual hides many Sabbat beneath the city, but the bargain carries a five-year deadline and a predatory cost.

- Spheres: Vampire, Demon
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Earthbound]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Burial Sites]]
- NPCs: [[Elijah]], [[Manishtusu]], [[Leila Monroe]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Explains why LA Sabbat vanish, then return empowered and traumatized in 2015.

### 1999 - The Yellow Springs Invades the Necropolis

The Yellow Springs campaign in the Los Angeles Necropolis collides with the Sixth Great Maelstrom and Avatar Storm. The invasion stalls, casualties mount, and Kuei-jin resources are trapped far from easy reinforcement.

- Spheres: Wraith, Kuei-jin, Mage
- Factions: [[Yellow Springs]], [[Crusaders]], [[Kuei-jin]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles Necropolis]], [[Yin World]]
- NPCs: None listed
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Nights of Prophecy]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Shows why the Kuei-jin turn from conquest to coalition politics.

### October 1999 - Hao Wei-Liang Falls

After military losses and political pressure, Hao Wei-Liang is forced into ritual suicide. His fall lets the Fence Menders reshape the campaign around controlled collaboration with selected Kin-jin.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin
- Factions: [[Fence Menders]], [[Quincunx]], [[Kuei-jin]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Shanghai]]
- NPCs: [[Hao Wei-Liang]], [[Jiejie Li]], [[Chiu Bao]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Marks the pivot from failed invasion to New Promise Mandarinate strategy.

### Late 1999 - The Divide-and-Rule Bargains

Kuei-jin diplomats approach Salvador Garcia and Louis Fortier with positions inside a new order. Their choices fracture Anarch unity and give the invaders local shock troops and legitimacy.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin, Vampire
- Factions: [[New Promise Mandarinate]], [[Anarchs]], [[El Hermandad]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Salvador Garcia]], [[Louis Fortier]], [[Monkey Trip Wu]], [[Fun Toy]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Turns Anarch exhaustion into the political death warrant of the Free State.

### New Year 2000 - The New Year's Riots End the Free State

The New Promise coalition uses chaos to crush or scatter holdouts. Refugees flee west coast cities, Lupines and scourges destroy many survivors, and Los Angeles becomes the Mandarinate's domain in practice.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin, Vampire, Werewolf
- Factions: [[New Promise Mandarinate]], [[Anarchs]], [[Kuei-jin]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[San Francisco]], [[San Diego]]
- NPCs: [[Monkey Trip Wu]], [[Fun Toy]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Anarchs Unbound]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Ends the Anarch Free State as a functioning political force in this continuity.

### 2000 - The Camarilla Names the Cathayan Problem

The Inner Circle studies the NPM as a strategic threat and authorizes containment. A Toreador justicar attempts negotiation, but Sabbat interference collapses the meeting and accelerates west coast destabilization.

- Spheres: Vampire, Kuei-jin
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[New Promise Mandarinate]], [[Sabbat]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[San Francisco]], [[American West]]
- NPCs: [[Sandhurst]]
- Sources: [[Shadow War]], [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: reconciled
- Impact: Explains why the Camarilla later returns to LA with policy, not just opportunism.

## Demon Reckoning and Devil's Night

**Range:** 2002

The Fallen and Earthbound reshape Los Angeles through apocalypse, faith, catastrophe, and Infernal Court politics. The Devil's Night earthquake turns the city into a beacon for supernatural claimants.

### 2002 - The Gates of Hell Open Toward Los Angeles

Escaped Fallen come to Los Angeles seeking the same cultural leverage that drew Lucifer. The city's Infernal Court forms around influence, faith, and the suspicion that the Morningstar is near.

- Spheres: Demon
- Factions: [[Fallen]], [[Infernal Court]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[Hollywood]]
- NPCs: [[Lucifer]], [[Spentu Mainyu]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Turns LA into a global Fallen pilgrimage site.

### 2002 - Devil's Night Breaks the City

Belial, Enshagkushanna, and Manishtusu's schemes culminate in a catastrophic earthquake and five days of civic collapse. The disaster feeds Earthbound plans until Lucifer's brief revelation interrupts the terror with mass religious awe.

- Spheres: Demon
- Factions: [[Earthbound]], [[Fallen]], [[Infernal Court]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[City Hall]], [[Catalina Island]]
- NPCs: [[Belial]], [[Enshagkushanna]], [[Manishtusu]], [[Lucifer]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Creates the major Demon-era rupture that future LA chronicles must account for.

### November-December 2002 - The Infernal Court Reorders Itself

Baroness Kishar escapes Hell, Lady Anat arrives, and Spentu Mainyu brokers authority within the Infernal Court and Blood Court. Demon politics become courtly, predatory, and deeply tied to LA influence networks.

- Spheres: Demon
- Factions: [[Infernal Court]], [[Blood Court]], [[Dragon Ministry]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Baroness Kishar]], [[Spentu Mainyu]], [[Lady Anat]]
- Sources: [[City of Angels]]
- Continuity: published
- Impact: Provides a standing Demon power structure for crossover citybook chapters.

With the ex-Anarchs broken and the NPM rising, the Camarilla moves into Los Angeles and names Sebastian LaCroix prince. Open war follows among the Tower, Sabbat, neo-Anarchs, and Kuei-jin around territory and the Ankaran Sarcophagus.

Kuei-jin agents and Camarilla traitors bring down Prince LaCroix. The conflict narrows into a harder struggle between neo-Anarch remnants and the NPM.

## Camarilla Return and Pillars of Salt Setup

**Range:** 2015

Camarilla strikes, Giovanni territorial claims, Sabbat re-emergence, and internal Camarilla betrayal create the opening conditions for the Pillars of Salt chronicle.

### 2015 - Archons Break the NPM

Camarilla Archons under Toreador and Tremere mandates launch lightning raids against the NPM, killing a major portion of its western vampire population. The Tower declares the city safe enough to invite new Kindred in.

- Spheres: Vampire, Kuei-jin
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Toreador]], [[Tremere]], [[New Promise Mandarinate]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]], [[American West]]
- NPCs: None listed
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Creates the immediate opening for the Pillars of Salt court.

### 2015 - Prince Perkins Takes a Shaky Throne

Prince Perkins of Clan Ventrue rises as controller of the domain, but his totalitarian instincts outrun his practical grasp on Los Angeles. The new court begins with authority and fragility intertwined.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Ventrue]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Prince Perkins]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Defines the first Camarilla ruler the chronicle reacts against.

### 2015 - The Giovanni Take Orange County

Clan Giovanni claims Orange County as a personal domain and names Bruno Giovanni as capo. Necromantic business interests become a formal territorial power beside the Camarilla domain.

- Spheres: Vampire, Wraith
- Factions: [[Giovanni]]
- Locations: [[Orange County]]
- NPCs: [[Bruno Giovanni]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt local notes]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Gives Orange County a distinct necromantic political identity.

### 2015 - The Sabbat Rise from the Burial Rite

The hidden Sabbat emerge from Elijah's old ritual as Manishtusu regains strength. Don Dominic De Guzman claims the archdiocese and tries to make Los Angeles worth holding for nomad packs.

## Five Years of Blood and Consequence

**Range:** 2016-2020

The first five years after the Camarilla return turn Los Angeles from a fresh conquest into a grinding multi-front war. The court changes hands, the Sabbat becomes organized again, the New Promise Mandarinate survives through its wu, and older supernatural systems force the city to admit that praxis is not the same thing as control.

### 2016 - Archon Bey's Report Names the War

The first serious outside review of the reclaimed domain describes Los Angeles as a war of years rather than months. Prince Pictor's praxis appears workable, but the court is short on trust, the Primogen barely know how to work together, and the city's enemies are better organized than the Tower wants to admit.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Sabbat]], [[New Promise Mandarinate]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Dafne Pictor]], [[Justicar Ulgahn Bey]], [[Prince Perkins]]
- Sources: [[Report for Archon Ulgin Bey]], [[Cleaned Pillars character dossiers]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Reframes the post-2015 domain as an attrition campaign instead of a completed Camarilla victory.

### 2016 - The Perkins Survey Becomes an Assassination Attempt

Perkins' survey of the domain is compromised by a Malkavian infiltrator, and Madeleine Pelletier discovers a copy with a list of targets. She tries to deliver what she found, but Perkins attempts to have her killed with Pictor as the instrument. Madeleine survives, turning the scandal into one of the first buried crimes of the new court.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Brujah]], [[Malkavian]], [[Tremere]], [[Ventrue]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Madeleine Pelletier]], [[Dafne Pictor]], [[Prince Perkins]]
- Sources: [[Report for Archon Ulgin Bey]], [[Cleaned Pillars character dossiers]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Explains why Pictor's rise carries more blood and compromise than the public court record admits, while preserving Madeleine as a living witness and political actor.

### 2016 - The Court Rebuilds Its Offices

The reclaimed court settles into a new administrative shape: Yuri serves as Seneschal, Judith de Leon as Harpy, Mehmed Bey as Keeper, Alexander Baird remains Sheriff, Lupo Salamanca serves as Scourge, and Cody West becomes Herald. Andy Watkins becomes the sort of field operator the domain needs more than it likes to admit, working beneath Baird's old-world authority.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Assamite]], [[Brujah]], [[Gangrel]], [[Malkavian]], [[Nosferatu]], [[Toreador]], [[Tremere]], [[Ventrue]]
- Locations: [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Yuri]], [[Judith de Leon]], [[Mehmed Bey]], [[Alexander Baird]], [[Lupo Salamanca]], [[Cody West]], [[Andy Watkins]]
- Sources: [[Report for Archon Ulgin Bey]], [[Cleaned Pillars character dossiers]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Marks the court's shift from emergency conquest to daily governance, with offices becoming leverage points for later intrigue.

### 2016 - San Diego Pushes Trouble North

Tara's San Diego continues to fray under paranoia, old Anarch resentment, and Cicatriz pressure. Her suspicion of Gangrel and other unreliable outsiders sends displaced Kindred, rumors, and useful grudges toward Los Angeles, where the new court is hungry for talent but wary of anything carrying San Diego's scent.

- Spheres: Vampire
- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Anarchs]], [[Gangrel]], [[Sabbat]]
- Locations: [[San Diego]], [[Los Angeles]]
- NPCs: [[Tara]], [[Cicatriz]], [[Wade "Trip" Carlson III]], [[Andy Watkins]]
- Sources: [[Pillars of Salt History of San Diego]], [[Cleaned Pillars character dossiers]]
- Continuity: pillars
- Impact: Connects Los Angeles' Gangrel and refugee politics to the instability of the southern domain.

### 2016 - The Sabbat Stops Being a Rumor

Don Dominic's archdiocese becomes a rallying point for surviving and arriving packs. The Liliaceae Lancers, the Answer, the Torrance Circle, Abramelin Oil, the Spear of Enoch, the Knights of St. Boris, La Petite Lunar, Aleph Null, and loose nomads give the Sword of Caine a real working shape again, even as Manishtusu's old burial debt remains unresolved beneath them.

- Spheres: Vampire, Demon
- Factions: [[Sabbat]], [[Earthbound]], [[Meester Van de Nacht]], [[The Answer]], [[Liliaceae Lancers]], [[The Torrance Circle]], [[Abramelin Oil]], [[Spear of Enoch]], [[The Knights of St. Boris]], [[La Petite Lunar]], [[Aleph Null]], [[Nomad Packs]]

### 2017 - The New Promise Survives Through Its Wu

The New Promise Mandarinate does not vanish after the Camarilla return. It contracts into disciplined wu and affiliated houses: the Harmonious Mender of the Broken Fence, Flatbush and Stockton Posse, the Caged Sun Brotherhood, House of the Salted Crown, the Sutra of Blackened Incense, and the Bright Lanterns Beneath the Overpass. The Mandarinate's public claim weakens, but its capacity for pressure remains.

- Spheres: Kuei-jin, Vampire
- Factions: [[New Promise Mandarinate]], [[Harmonious Mender of the Broken Fence]], [[Flatbush and Stockton Posse]], [[The Caged Sun Brotherhood]], [[House of the Salted Crown]], [[The Sutra of Blackened Incense]], [[Bright Lanterns Beneath the Overpass]]

### 2017 - The Nosferatu Leave Gaps in the Wires

Nictuku rumors force the Nosferatu to look inward and below, leaving corridors of digital intelligence strangely open. Deena HaLevi moves into the vacuum with security systems, surveillance webs, and technomantic study, while Jackie the Tail and Kirill Levonoff harden older information networks against something hunting their clan.

- Spheres: Vampire

### 2017 - The Glass Walkers Break and the Under-City Answers

Black Spiral Dancers beneath Griffith Park shatter much of the Glass Walker network before the city's other defenders understand the scale of the attack. Ratkin, Corax, and Rokea become the practical early-warning system: rats in drains, ravens above disasters, and shark-folk striking where the city poisons the sea.

- Spheres: Werewolf, Changing Breeds

### 2018 - The Dead Become the City's Second Currency

The Sixth Great Maelstrom's damage continues to pay dividends for the Giovanni. Don Bruno consolidates Orange County while Serafina Giovanni makes herself useful inside Los Angeles proper. Wraiths, Guilds, and Hierarchy officers bargain with monsters because the old dead systems no longer protect them reliably.

### 2018 - Durriken Paints the Hidden Jyhad

Durriken's visions move from general Gehenna dread toward a specific theory: demons are arranging Los Angeles' supernatural factions into collisions. As a Harpy, he uses reputation, paintings, and suggestive questions to make the court discover pieces of the pattern itself, while his private trail toward Melvin Schact draws him deeper into Malkavian prophecy.

- Spheres: Vampire, Demon, Mage, Changeling, Werewolf

### 2019 - The Technocracy Treats LA as a Contamination Event

The Technocratic Union tightens its Los Angeles systems through Disneyland, JPL, and Sony. Umano Sallase's people tag, categorize, contain, or erase supernatural incidents before they become consensus damage. Vampires still call it Masquerade maintenance, but the city is increasingly protected by mortals who would rather vivisect the monsters than bargain with them.

- Spheres: Mage, Vampire, Demon, Werewolf
- Factions: [[Technocracy]], [[Syndicate]], [[New World Order]], [[Camarilla]]

### 2019 - The JPL Thread Crosses the Mage Chronicle

Local mage activity brushes directly against the Los Angeles crisis: impossible phones, mirror-blood anomalies, strange scans at UCLA, a vampire attack on JPL, and Janet's eventual entry into the Technocratic Union. Marcus Willows offers ritual knowledge from a hidden path, while Oliver is identified as a reality deviant.

### By 2020 - Calvin Olsen Is Named Seneschal

By the five-year mark, Calvin Olsen is named Seneschal of Los Angeles. Pictor's Tremere praxis remains the public center of the domain, but Ventrue institutional power returns to the machinery of court through Calvin's office. The change does not end the war; it changes the court's preferred language for fighting it.

- Factions: [[Camarilla]], [[Ventrue]], [[Tremere]]

### 2020 - The Central Orange County Bargain

Sir Alexander Goodwin and the fae politics around Disneyland pull vampires, hunters, Technocrats, and Sabbat targets into the same negotiation space. Central Orange County, the Santa Monica freehold, the Santa Monica Pier prophecy, JPL's Technocrats, and the Crystal Cathedral all become pieces in a bargain no single faction fully understands.
